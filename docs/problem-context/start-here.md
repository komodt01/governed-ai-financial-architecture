# Problem Context

Artificial intelligence is increasingly being integrated into financial systems from fraud detection and exception handling to intelligent routing and operational automation.

However, regulated payment platforms operate under structural constraints that differ from most AI-enabled environments.

These systems must preserve:

- Deterministic transaction execution  
- Financial record integrity  
- Full audit traceability  
- Segregation of duties  
- Regulatory accountability  

AI introduces probabilistic behavior into systems designed for predictable and accountable outcomes.

The architectural challenge is therefore not whether AI can improve operational efficiency.  
The challenge is how to integrate AI capabilities without weakening the deterministic guarantees that financial systems depend on.

This reference model explores how AI can operate as an advisory capability layer within a controlled transaction architecture.

The goal is to demonstrate how modern AI capabilities can coexist with the governance, compliance, and audit expectations of regulated payment environments.
