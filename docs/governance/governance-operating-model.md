# Governance & Operating Model

Architecture defines structure.  

Governance defines accountability.

In regulated payment environments, AI integration is not simply a technical enhancement it is an operating model decision.

This model clarifies how authority, oversight, and accountability are structured so that AI augments operations without compromising financial correctness or regulatory compliance.

---

## Architectural Principles

AI is advisory by default.  
Financial state changes remain deterministic and policy-bound.

AI systems:
- May classify, summarize, or recommend
- May surface risk indicators
- May assist case triage
- May highlight anomalies or patterns

AI systems do **not**:
- Commit ledger entries
- Override policy enforcement
- Execute financial state changes autonomously

All material decisions remain attributable to accountable roles.

---

## Decision Rights & Review Model

AI introduces a new class of decisions that must be explicitly governed.

The operating model distinguishes between:

### Operational Case Decisions
Routine exception handling and workflow actions.
Owned by operations teams.
Auditable through case logs and evidence capture.

### Financial State Changes
Any action that alters balances, settlement status, or accounting records.
Subject to dual control or deterministic policy enforcement.

### Policy or Threshold Changes
Adjustments to rules, risk thresholds, routing logic, or model inputs.
Subject to formal change management and documented approval.

Authority is separated from execution to preserve independence of control.

---

## AI Lifecycle & Change Control

AI capabilities evolve over time. Governance must evolve with them.

The lifecycle includes:

### Model Introduction
- Defined scope and intended use
- Risk classification and materiality assessment
- Approval by governance authority

### Threshold or Policy Updates
- Documented rationale
- Impact assessment
- Controlled rollout

### Model Updates or Retraining
- Performance validation
- Drift analysis
- Evidence retention for audit

AI changes follow the same rigor as policy changes.

---

## Override & Drift Review

Human override activity is not failure it is governance signal.

The operating model requires:
- Tracking override frequency
- Monitoring model performance variance
- Reviewing bias or drift indicators
- Periodic governance review sessions

Oversight focuses on sustained control, not one-time validation.

---

## Accountability Map

Every AI-enabled workflow must answer:

- Who owns the model?
- Who approves its scope?
- Who monitors performance?
- Who reviews overrides?
- Who is accountable under audit?

Governance responsibility must be explicit before deployment not inferred after incident.

---

## Architectural Intent

The objective is not to slow innovation.

The objective is to ensure:

- Deterministic financial integrity
- Clear separation of duties
- Transparent decision attribution
- Audit-ready evidence trails
- Sustainable AI augmentation

Without defined governance, AI introduces ambiguity.

With structured governance, AI becomes a controlled capability layer within the enterprise architecture.
